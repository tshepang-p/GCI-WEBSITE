What is green campus initiative?

Green Campus Initiative, is a student organisation within the University of Cape Town. It aims to make UCT a sustainable and environmentally friendly institution. It was started towards the end of 2007 by Susan Botha, who had been working for the Namaqualand Restoration Initiative at UCT.

The Green Campus Initiative (GCI) is a movement or program focused on transforming educational institutions into more sustainable, eco-conscious, and environmentally responsible spaces. It involves students, faculty, staff, and partners in reducing environmental impact and promoting sustainable living on campus.

Our pillars of sustainability
and eco-friendliness

Resource Efficiency

Reducing pollution in air, water, and soil helps protect the environment and public health. Conserving biodiversity and natural ecosystems, along with promoting renewable energy, supports a more sustainable future. Minimizing waste through recycling and reuse further reduces environmental impact.

Environmental Protection

Eco-friendly practices benefit local communities by improving access to clean air and water, promoting health and well-being. Involving people in sustainable habits and ensuring fair labor and ethical sourcing also support a more just and environmentally responsible society.

Social Responsibility

Eco-friendly practices support local communities by promoting clean air and water, which enhance health and well-being. Educating and involving people in sustainable habits encourages long-term positive change, while fair labor and ethical sourcing ensure that environmental efforts also respect human rights and dignity.

Environmental Education & Awareness

Promoting sustainability through c ampaigns and school programs helps instill eco-friendly values from an early age. Encouraging daily habits like saving water and reducing plastic use, along with supporting community clean-ups and green initiatives, fosters collective action. Raising awareness about climate change, deforestation, and biodiversity loss further strengthens the commitment to protecting the environment.

Economic Viability

Supporting green jobs and sustainable industries helps drive economic growth while protecting the environment. Making eco-friendly choices affordable and practical encourages wider adoption, and investing in sustainable innovation and infrastructure builds a foundation for the future. These efforts create long-term value without harming the planet.

Sustainable Design & Innovation

Eco-friendly architecture, packaging, and product design help reduce environmental impact through thoughtful, sustainable choices. Innovations like biomimicry and green technology further support this shift, while using low-impact materials and processes ensures a lighter footprint on the planet.
